Frank Sinatra

The man who enthralled billions

Time Line of Frank Sinatra's life:

  • December 1915 - Born in Hoboken, New Jersey, US
  • 1935 - Frank Sinatra’s first big break came when he and a local trio who called themselves the Three Flashes auditioned separately for an appearance on Major Bowes and His Original Amateur Hour. They put them all together and called them the Hoboken Four.
  • 1939 - Frank Sinatra married his childhood sweetheart, Nancy Barbato, on February 4.
  • 1939 - Frank Sinatra cut his first record with Harry James (trumpeter), “From The Bottom of My Heart” (backed with “Melancholy Mood”) on the Brunswick label. Recorded at 78 rpm, neither song hit the charts.
  • 1940 - Sinatra joined the Tommy Dorsey Orchestra, which significantly boosted his career.
  • 1941 - His popularity soared, leading to the term "Sinatramania" as he became a teen idol.
  • 1943 - Sinatra signed a contract with Columbia Records and began his solo career.
  • 1944 - Began shooting Anchors Aweigh with musical-comedy superstar Gene Kelly. Frank Sinatra Jr. was born.
  • 1946 - “The House I Live In” starring FS wins the Honorary Award at the Academy Awards.
  • 1947 - Frank Sinatra co-starred with Kathryn Grayson, Jimmy Durante, and Peter Lawford in MGM’s It Happened in Brooklyn.
  • 1948 - Christina “Tina” Sinatra is born on Father’s Day. Production began on Take Me Out to the Ball Game at MGM with Frank Sinatra and Gene Kelly.
  • 1951 - Frank Sinatra made his first appearance in Las Vegas at the Desert Inn.
  • 1953 - FS signed with Capitol Records.
  • 1954 - Wins the Oscar for Best Supporting Actor for “From Here To Eternity.”
  • 1955 - Starred in "The Man with the Golden Arm," earning an Academy Award nomination for Best Actor.
  • 1955 - Capitol released “Songs for Young Lovers,” an album that included classics like "I Get a Kick Out of You,” “A Foggy Day,” and "My Funny Valentine."
  • 1959 - FS won 3 Grammy Awards for “Come Dance With Me!”: Album of the Year, Best Male Vocal Performance, and Special Award: Artists & Repertoire Contribution.
  • 1960 - “Summit at the Sands”: The Rat Pack – Joey Bishop, Sammy Davis Jr., Dean Martin, and Peter Lawford – begin their legendary stint at the Sands hotel in Las Vegas.
  • 1961 - "Come Swing With Me!" is released.
  • 1962 - “The Manchurian Candidate” releases starring Sinatra, Laurence Harvey.
  • 1963 - Sinatra’s son, Frank Sinatra Jr., was kidnapped from Harrah's Lake Tahoe and held for ransom before being released.
  • 1965 - Frank organized a “summit meeting” of the Rat Pack in St. Louis as a benefit for Dismas House of St. Louis.
  • 1966 - FS wins 3 Grammy Awards, including Album of the Year for “September of My Years.”
  • 1966 - Released "Strangers in the Night," which won multiple Grammy Awards, including Record of the Year.
  • 1968 - Recorded “My Way.”
  • 1971 - Announced his retirement from performing but returned to the stage two years later.
  • 1974 - "The Main Event" was staged at Madison Square Garden and broadcast live.
  • 1979 - FS celebrates 40 years in show business on his 64th birthday with a huge celebration at Caesar’s Palace in Las Vegas.
  • 1980 - Released "Trilogy: Past Present Future," featuring the hit "Theme from New York, New York."
  • 1982 - In the Dominican Republic, he gave a concert later shown on U.S. television as The Concert for the Americas.
  • 1984 - FS and Quincy Jones joined to record the album "L.A. Is My Lady."
  • 1985 - Awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Ronald Reagan.
  • 1994 - "Duets" went multi-platinum.
  • 1995 - "Duets" certified 3x multi-platinum; "Duets II" certified platinum.
  • 1997 - Made his last public performance on February 25.
  • 1998 - The world loses an icon. Frank Sinatra passed away on May 14 in Los Angeles at age 82.

Frank Sinatra’s legacy continues to shine as one of the greatest entertainers of all time. His smooth voice, charismatic presence, and timeless music have left an indelible mark on the world. From his early days as a crooner to his reign as a Hollywood star and leader of the Rat Pack, Sinatra's influence spans generations. His music remains a symbol of elegance and emotion, and his impact on popular culture is immeasurable. As we remember Frank Sinatra, we celebrate a legend whose voice will never fade.

-- Paul Martin
